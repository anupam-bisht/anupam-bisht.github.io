A Student Guide

I have received multiple messages through known and unknown contacts over the months with questions about living, studying and managing as a student. In this section, I list some of the things. Feel free to contact me if you need some extra opinions. I will keep addding and removing stuff as required. Note: Most information will be based on my experinces in University of Calgary and Calgary city. Also, please take everything here with a pinch of salt. Details will give you an idea, but I have no gaurantee will work or be exact.

Applying for studies as a Thesis based student: How do I start?

  • Select a program/department.
  • Shortlist supervisors based on your interest. Tip: Try to broaden your interest.
  • Write a good email. Remind after 1-2 weeks if required.Note: Email should have a clear subject.Including details about your interests and why specifically you are interested in the lab will improve the quality of the email.

Questions about Immigrations, finance document, etc.

Best way is to contact your university. At the university of Calgary there is a service called Immigration Services offered by the ISS - International Student Services.

After coming to Canada: Important to Stuff to do

  • Social Insurace Number(SIN): Can be done by going to Service Canada centers many around the city of Calagry. Nearest to university is at Banff Trail.
  • Alberta Health Card :(By going to a nearby registry, nearest to University : In Lions Park)
  • Alberta ID Card:(not necessary, but good to have): required if you want a id proof with photo, date of birth, address.

Look for housing

Housing in Calagry can be easily found through website such as Rentfaster or Kijiji. Tip: Try finding a house nearby the university or bit far with a transit (preferably the LRT).

Transit in Calgary

  • Transit in Calgary is mainly Busses and the Light Rail Transit - LRT (similar to Metro in India/U-Bahn in Germany). The university (rather the government) has made a scheme (simialr to buspass for students), something called UPASS, which is a sticker in the ID-Card. It allows you to ride in any of the busses or LRTs. We pay for it in the fees.
  • In case you do not have the UPASS you can purchase a ticket from some stores, online,or the LRT station. You can use same ticket you get from the LRT in the bus. Check website of the Calgary Transit for more upto date information. Typical ticket valid for 90 mins is about $3.5. However, day passes are also available.
  • One can also apply for the Low income pass , however it needs one to file taxes. The application requires us to prove that our income is below a certain limit. One way is through the document which is produced during filing taxes.
